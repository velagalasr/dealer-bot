Caterpillar Dealer Service – Comprehensive FAQ
________________________________________
1. How do I schedule a service for my machine?
You can schedule service by contacting your local Cat® dealer’s service department by phone, email, or through the online service portal. Provide your machine’s serial number, hours, and location. Many dealers also offer remote scheduling via My.Cat.com or the Cat app.
________________________________________
2. What information should I provide when requesting service?
Please include:
•	Machine model and serial number
•	Total operating hours
•	Description of the problem or required maintenance
•	Location of the equipment
•	Preferred contact method and available service time
Providing accurate details allows the dealer to send the right technician, tools, and parts.
________________________________________
3. How do I find the correct dealer for my location?
Visit cat.com/findadealer and enter your ZIP/postal code. The tool displays your authorized dealer, contact details, and service centers near you.
________________________________________
4. What should I do if my machine breaks down unexpectedly?
Contact your dealer’s emergency service line immediately. Provide your machine details and fault symptoms. The dealer can dispatch a field technician or arrange transport to the nearest branch. Some dealers also provide 24/7 mobile service for critical equipment.
________________________________________
5. What does a standard preventive maintenance (PM) service include?
A PM service typically covers:
•	Engine oil and filter replacement
•	Fuel, air, and hydraulic filter checks
•	Cooling system inspection
•	Lubrication of joints and linkages
•	Safety inspections
•	Diagnostic code scan and reset
•	Review of telematics data (if available)
________________________________________
6. How often should I perform preventive maintenance?
Follow the interval recommended in your Operation & Maintenance Manual (usually every 250–500 hours). Machines used in harsh environments (dust, extreme cold/heat) may need shorter intervals. Telematics systems can help track real-time maintenance schedules.
________________________________________
7. Can I get service performed at my job site?
Yes. Most Cat dealers offer field service or mobile maintenance vans equipped to perform on-site repairs and inspections, reducing downtime and transportation costs.
________________________________________
8. Is emergency 24/7 service available?
Yes, many authorized dealers provide round-the-clock emergency response for breakdowns. A callout fee may apply depending on distance and urgency.
________________________________________
9. How fast can a technician arrive after I request service?
Response time varies by region and workload, but emergency calls are prioritized. Dealers aim to reach breakdown sites within a few hours for critical assets.
________________________________________
10. What is the difference between regular service and major overhaul?
•	Regular service maintains system performance (oil, filters, inspections).
•	Major overhaul involves full teardown and reconditioning of major components (engine, transmission, hydraulics) to extend life cycle.
________________________________________
11. How do I track service and maintenance history?
All service records are stored in the dealer’s ERP system and visible to customers through My.Cat.com or VisionLink®. You can also request printed service summaries.
________________________________________
12. What are my warranty options?
New machines typically come with a standard 12-month warranty. Dealers offer Extended Protection Plans (EPP) and Customer Value Agreements (CVAs) that cover parts, labor, and maintenance for longer terms.
________________________________________
13. What if the warranty has expired?
You can still receive full dealer service. The dealer will provide an estimate and may recommend an extended coverage plan to protect future repairs.
________________________________________
14. How do I get a cost estimate before service?
The dealer’s service advisor will review your request and generate a quote based on labor hours, parts, and travel. For PMs, standard flat-rate packages are available.
________________________________________
15. Are only genuine Cat parts used?
Yes, authorized dealers use genuine Cat® parts or Cat Reman components that meet the same factory specifications. Using genuine parts preserves reliability and warranty coverage.
________________________________________
16. What’s the difference between Cat Reman and new parts?
Cat Reman parts are factory-reconditioned to OEM standards, tested, and come with the same warranty as new parts. They offer excellent value and sustainability benefits.
________________________________________
17. How do I order parts if my machine is down?
Use Parts.Cat.com, the dealer’s portal, or call the parts counter directly. Many dealers offer same-day or next-day delivery for stocked items.
________________________________________
18. What is the benefit of scheduled oil sampling (SOS)?
Cat’s Scheduled Oil Sampling helps detect early signs of wear, contamination, or coolant leaks. This predictive maintenance tool helps prevent costly failures.
________________________________________
19. Can I integrate telematics data into my maintenance plan?
Yes, VisionLink® and My.Cat.com integrate machine health, alerts, and usage to automatically schedule PMs, send reminders, and optimize downtime.
________________________________________
20. How are safety inspections handled?
Every service includes basic safety checks—brakes, seat belts, warning lights, backup alarms, and emergency systems. Dealers also offer annual safety certification inspections.
________________________________________
21. How is machine software updated?
Technicians use factory-approved Cat Electronic Technician (Cat ET) software to update firmware and calibrations during service visits.
________________________________________
22. Can the dealer perform calibration or diagnostics remotely?
Some models support remote diagnostics or remote flash updates through telematics if connectivity is enabled.
________________________________________
23. Can I get operator training as part of my service plan?
Yes. Dealers offer on-site or classroom training on machine operation, maintenance, and safety procedures to help improve uptime and reduce misuse.
________________________________________
24. How do I know when filters or oil need changing?
Follow the intervals in your machine’s manual or monitor via telematics. The system alerts you when maintenance is due.
________________________________________
25. What are signs that my machine needs urgent repair?
•	Visible fluid leaks
•	Power loss or abnormal noise
•	Warning lights or diagnostic codes
•	Excessive smoke or vibration
•	Hydraulic lag or overheating
Contact your dealer immediately if you notice any of these.
________________________________________
26. What happens if I skip scheduled maintenance?
Neglecting service can lead to premature wear, breakdowns, voided warranties, and higher long-term costs. Dealers strongly recommend staying compliant with PM schedules.
________________________________________
27. How can I extend the life of my machine?
Perform regular PM, use genuine Cat filters and fluids, store equipment properly, and train operators to minimize idling and misuse.
________________________________________
28. What is a Customer Value Agreement (CVA)?
A CVA is a flexible maintenance contract covering scheduled PMs, parts delivery, remote monitoring, and service at predictable costs.
________________________________________
29. What are the benefits of a CVA?
Predictable costs, improved uptime, genuine parts, reduced administrative effort, and machine health monitoring with automated service alerts.
________________________________________
30. Do you offer fleet management assistance?
Yes. Dealers can provide reports on fleet utilization, idle time, fault trends, and upcoming maintenance to optimize performance and budget.
________________________________________
31. What’s the difference between reactive and preventive maintenance?
•	Reactive: Fixing problems after failure.
•	Preventive: Servicing components before failure to prevent downtime.
Dealers recommend preventive approaches for high-value assets.
________________________________________
32. Can you service non-Cat attachments or tools?
Yes, dealers often service attachments (e.g., buckets, hammers) if compatible. However, warranty coverage applies only to Cat equipment.
________________________________________
33. How can I monitor my machine remotely?
By subscribing to Cat Product Link™, which provides real-time machine data, GPS location, utilization hours, and alerts accessible via VisionLink.
________________________________________
34. How do I handle service at remote job sites?
Field service units are equipped for remote diagnostics, oil changes, and emergency repairs. Dealers also use satellite connectivity for telematics.
________________________________________
35. What is condition-based maintenance (CBM)?
CBM relies on sensors and SOS reports to trigger maintenance based on actual machine condition rather than fixed hours.
________________________________________
36. How do I ensure compliance with environmental regulations?
Dealers handle used oil and filters in accordance with EPA and local regulations. Waste disposal certificates can be issued upon request.
________________________________________
37. Can I track service costs per machine?
Yes, cost summaries are available per unit via My.Cat.com or dealer reports to assist in budgeting and ROI analysis.
________________________________________
38. What financing options are available for large repairs?
You can use a Cat® Card, extended terms, or equipment finance programs for major repairs and rebuilds.
________________________________________
39. What is the rebuild program?
Cat Certified Rebuilds restore used machines to like-new condition, including component replacement, testing, and new-machine warranty.
________________________________________
40. How long does a rebuild take?
A Certified Rebuild typically takes 6–10 weeks depending on model and parts availability.
________________________________________
41. Can you provide loaner or rental machines during repair?
Yes, most dealers offer short-term rentals to minimize downtime during major repairs.
________________________________________
42. How do I prepare my machine for service?
Ensure it’s clean, accessible, and located in a safe area with keys available. Report any fault codes or unusual performance beforehand.
________________________________________
43. Can I track technician arrival status?
Many dealers now send text/email updates with technician ETA and job status.
________________________________________
44. Do you handle both mechanical and electronic diagnostics?
Yes. Dealers use Cat ET and diagnostic hardware for all mechanical, hydraulic, and ECM systems.
________________________________________
45. What if a service issue reoccurs soon after repair?
Contact the service advisor. Repeat issues are reviewed under warranty or goodwill adjustments depending on cause.

51. How do I find the service manual for a specific CAT model?
You can access service manuals through the SIS2.0 (Service Information System) using your dealer login. Each model’s serial number prefix must be entered to retrieve the correct service manual, schematics, and testing procedures.
________________________________________
52. Can I download SIS content for offline use?
Yes, SIS2.0 supports offline content synchronization for authorized users. You can pre-download selected model documentation to use during field service where internet access is limited.
________________________________________
53. How can I check if a machine has open service campaigns?
Log in to Warranty & Service Campaign Portal (WSCP) or Product Link. Enter the serial number to view any open service letters, Product Improvement Programs (PIPs), or mandatory updates.
________________________________________
54. What’s the correct procedure for reporting a recurring fault code?
Use CAT ET (Electronic Technician) to capture the code snapshot, verify parameters, and check historical logs. Submit the Product Problem Report (PPR) through the Dealer Solution Network (DSN) with complete diagnostics and logs attached.
________________________________________
55. How do I calibrate sensors after replacement?
Refer to the Testing and Adjusting section of the service manual. Calibration steps vary by model but typically require CAT ET connection, specific warm-up parameters, and recording zero-offset readings.
________________________________________
56. How can I update machine ECM firmware?
Firmware updates are done using CAT Flash File Utility via CAT ET. Download the latest flash files from SIS Web Services corresponding to your model’s serial prefix. Always back up configuration files before flashing.
________________________________________
57. What’s the process to verify a machine’s maintenance history?
Access VisionLink, Product Link, or Dealer Business System (DBS). These show service logs, preventive maintenance schedules, and dealer-entered service records tied to the machine’s Product Identification Number (PIN).
________________________________________
58. Can dealers perform field ECM reprogramming?
Yes, authorized field technicians can perform ECM reprogramming using a CAT Communication Adapter (Comm 3 or Comm 4) and CAT ET, provided the machine’s battery voltage and connection stability meet minimum thresholds.
________________________________________
59. What are common causes of ECM communication loss?
Loose connectors, damaged CAN wiring, low voltage, or incorrect termination resistors. Run a continuity and voltage drop test before replacing ECMs.
________________________________________
60. How to request engineering assistance for unresolved issues?
Submit a Dealer Solution Network (DSN) case with logs, CAT ET data, oil analysis reports, and photos. If needed, escalate through Technical Communicator (TC) to reach product engineering.
________________________________________
61. How is machine downtime calculated for warranty claims?
Downtime begins when a failure is reported and ends upon repair completion. Dealers must record exact timestamps in the claim notes; Caterpillar audits downtime records during warranty review.
________________________________________
62. Can dealers install non-CAT parts during warranty repairs?
No, only genuine CAT parts may be used on warranty claims. Use of third-party components may void the warranty unless pre-approved through a Nonstandard Repair Authorization (NSRA).
________________________________________
63. What’s the standard response time for warranty claim approval?
Typically 3–5 business days, depending on completeness and complexity. Claims missing failure photos or component analysis may take longer.
________________________________________
64. How do dealers dispose of failed components under warranty?
Retain all failed components for Caterpillar Return Inspection (CRI). Parts flagged for return will appear in the Parts Return Authorization (PRA) system. Ship back within the specified timeframe.
________________________________________
65. Can a dealer perform warranty repairs on a different dealer’s machine?
Yes, with cross-dealer authorization from Caterpillar Warranty Administration. The servicing dealer must include a Cross Dealer Repair (CDR) code in the claim.
________________________________________
66. How are reman parts handled in service jobs?
Reman (remanufactured) parts follow the Core Return Policy. The dealer must return the used core within 30 days to receive credit. Track returns through the Core Management System (CMS).
________________________________________
67. How do I find equivalent Reman part numbers?
Use the Reman Part Finder Tool available in Parts.Cat.Com or SIS2.0. Enter the original part number to view the Reman equivalent, if available.
________________________________________
68. How often are technical bulletins updated?
Service Information Bulletins (SIBs) are released weekly. Dealers receive email notifications or can subscribe to automatic updates in DSN/SIS.
________________________________________
69. Where can I get hydraulic schematic diagrams?
In SIS2.0, open your model, go to “Schematics,” and select “Hydraulic.” Diagrams are downloadable in PDF or interactive SVG formats for easier zooming and layer filtering.
________________________________________
70. How to identify superseded part numbers?
Use Parts.Cat.Com or SIS Part Supersession Tool. Superseded parts are marked as “Replaced By” with the latest valid part number.
________________________________________
71. Can I perform a root cause analysis through Cat Inspect?
Cat Inspect allows fault tracking and condition reporting, but root cause analysis must be done manually using Cat ET data, fluid analysis, and physical inspection results.
________________________________________
72. What is the typical lead time for service parts?
Varies by region — typically 1–3 business days for standard parts, and 5–10 days for special orders. Critical downtime orders can be expedited under Emergency Order (EO) priority.
________________________________________
73. Can I request training for my service technicians?
Yes, training can be requested via CAT University or through your Regional Dealer Learning Advisor. Courses cover electrical diagnostics, hydraulics, emissions, and telematics.
________________________________________
74. What’s the correct oil sampling frequency for high-hour machines?
Every 250 service hours for standard duty, or every 125 hours for severe duty. Oil analysis results should be uploaded to S.O.S. Services for trend analysis.
________________________________________
75. How to handle fluid contamination issues?
Immediately isolate the machine, collect samples for S.O.S. analysis, replace filters, and flush the system if contamination exceeds threshold. Update service logs in DBS.
________________________________________
76. Can dealers track warranty parts shipment status?
Yes, through Dealer Warranty Portal → PRA → Shipment Tracking, which integrates with FedEx/Caterpillar Logistics systems.
________________________________________
77. Are software licenses for Cat ET transferable between users?
No. Each license is tied to a specific user and workstation ID. To reassign, contact Dealer Systems Admin for a transfer request.
________________________________________
78. How to verify Product Link connectivity issues?
Check the antenna, SIM card, and GPS module power. Log in to VisionLink → Asset Monitoring → Connectivity Report to identify last communication timestamp.
________________________________________
79. What’s the standard battery voltage for telematics modules?
Typically 9–32V DC input. A drop below 9V can cause intermittent connectivity or data loss.
________________________________________
80. How do I generate a service quotation for a customer?
Use DBS Service Quoting Module. Select labor operation codes, parts, and miscellaneous charges. Validate against CAT Flat Rate (CFR) guidelines before approval.
________________________________________
81. Can dealers integrate service data with CRM systems?
Yes, through APIs provided in Dealer Integration Gateway (DIG), which allows CRM platforms to sync service orders, equipment details, and warranty claims.
________________________________________
82. How are customer satisfaction surveys handled?
Post-service, the system triggers an NPS (Net Promoter Score) survey via email or SMS. Dealer management monitors feedback dashboards in Customer Experience Portal.
________________________________________
83. Can warranty coverage be extended?
Yes. Customers can purchase Extended Protection Plans (EPP) before the standard warranty expires. Dealers must complete eligibility checks in Warranty Administration System (WAS).
________________________________________
84. How do dealers handle software obsolescence?
Dealers receive obsolescence notifications in Dealer Flash File Portal and must upgrade service laptops with the latest CAT ET versions within 30 days.

85. What’s the retention period for service job documentation?

86. How to validate calibration certificates for torque tools?
Certificates must be renewed annually and stored in Dealer Quality Assurance (DQA) system. Field technicians should verify calibration status before use.

87. How are service KPIs tracked?

________________________________________
88. What is the process to handle environmental spills during service?

89. Can mobile service units be tracked?
Yes, using VisionLink Fleet Management. It monitors technician vehicles for job scheduling, GPS tracking, and idle time analysis.
________________________________________
90. How to verify technician certification levels?
Use Dealer Technician Qualification Database (DTQD). Certifications are categorized as Level 1 (Basic), Level 2 (Advanced), and Level 3 (Master Tech).

91. What is the CAT standard for customer response time?
Field service response should occur within 4 hours for critical breakdowns and within 24 hours for normal service requests.

92. Can a dealer view competitor part substitutions?
No, Caterpillar restricts competitor cross-references. Only validated CAT or Reman part supersessions are available through authorized systems.

93. How are claims audited by Caterpillar?
Warranty audits are conducted annually. Dealers are evaluated on accuracy, timeliness, and adherence to Service Letter (SL) compliance.

94. How to request product training materials for new launches?
Available in Dealer Launch Portal (DLP) under “Product Support Resources.” Dealers can download presentation kits, video tutorials, and quick-start guides.
________________________________________
95. Can dealers submit feedback on diagnostic tools?
Yes, through the Dealer Feedback Program (DFP). Submissions are reviewed by Caterpillar’s Tooling & Diagnostics engineering team.
________________________________________
96. What’s the typical turnaround for oil analysis reports?
24–48 hours after sample receipt. Critical results trigger automatic email alerts to the service manager.
________________________________________
97. How are safety recalls communicated to dealers?
Caterpillar issues a Product Safety Bulletin (PSB) and updates SIS2.0 → Product Notices. Dealers must acknowledge receipt and confirm completion.

98. Can I access customer equipment utilization reports?
Yes, through VisionLink → Productivity Dashboard, showing idle vs. work hours, fuel consumption, and production metrics.
________________________________________
99. How often should telematics firmware be updated?
At least once per quarter or as per the Product Link Software Bulletin. Automatic OTA (Over-The-Air) updates can be configured for active devices.
________________________________________
100. How are unresolved dealer disputes handled?
Escalate via Dealer Support Network (DSN) to the Regional Dealer Manager (RDM). If unresolved, cases can be reviewed under Dealer Governance Council (DGC).
101. What diagnostic tools support predictive maintenance for Cat equipment?
Predictive maintenance integrates Cat Asset Intelligence, Product Link™, and VisionLink Predictive. These systems use telematics and fluid-analysis data to forecast component wear and automatically generate work orders in Dealer Business System (DBS) once thresholds are exceeded.
________________________________________
102. How is machine health score calculated in VisionLink?
The health score combines sensor data (temperature, pressure, duty-cycle), event severity, open fault codes, and overdue maintenance hours. The algorithm assigns weighted values and generates a percentage score that technicians use to prioritize service.
________________________________________
103. Can AI help detect early failure patterns?
Yes. Caterpillar Analytics Hub runs ML models on historical service and S.O.S. data to identify failure precursors (e.g., injector imbalance, turbo speed deviation). Dealers can subscribe to proactive alert feeds through Dealer Integration Gateway (DIG) APIs.
________________________________________
104. How does Cat Inspect use AI image recognition?
When technicians upload photos, Cat Inspect’s vision model checks for surface cracks, leaks, or corrosion patterns, flags anomalies, and pre-fills defect categories in the inspection report—reducing manual entry time by 40 %.
________________________________________
105. What’s the correct workflow for emission-related diagnostics?
Use Cat ET v2024 or later with the Emissions Tool Pack. Capture DPF soot load, NOx conversion efficiency, and DEF quality. Upload logs to Caterpillar Emissions Portal (CEP) for validation against EPA Tier 4/Stage V parameters.
________________________________________
106. How do I handle DPF regeneration failures?
Verify temperature sensors and differential-pressure sensor health, perform a manual regeneration via Cat ET, then submit results to CEP. Persistent failures require ECM flash-update using the latest emission-calibrated file.
________________________________________
107. Can remote technicians initiate service diagnostics?
Yes. Remote Troubleshoot (RTS) lets certified technicians connect securely to the machine’s ECM through Product Link, read codes, and perform parameter overrides without dispatching a field tech.
________________________________________
108. What is the latency of remote diagnostics?
Average 5–20 seconds per command depending on cellular coverage and VPN latency. RTS encrypts data using TLS 1.3 with CAT PKI certificates to maintain end-to-end security.
________________________________________
109. How are firmware updates pushed remotely?
Through Remote Flash Service in VisionLink. Machines receive the flash file over the air once connected to Product Link; progress is logged in Dealer Fleet Monitor (DFM).
________________________________________
110. How to verify successful remote flash?
After completion, RTS auto-generates a checksum verification and posts the hash in Service History Record (SHR) within DBS. Technicians must review and mark “Verified OK.”
________________________________________
111. What’s the role of the Dealer Integration Gateway (DIG)?
DIG provides REST APIs allowing external CRM, ERP, or predictive-analytics tools to interact with Caterpillar systems—enabling automatic work-order creation, part availability checks, and telematics data streaming.
________________________________________
112. Can I integrate dealer mobile apps with DIG?
Yes, provided apps implement OAuth 2.0 authorization and comply with CAT’s API Security Policy. Developers obtain credentials through Developer.Cat.Com Portal.
________________________________________
113. How does Caterpillar secure telematics data?
Data in transit uses TLS 1.3; at rest, AES-256 encryption. Product Link devices employ hardware TPM modules for device authentication and cryptographic key storage.
________________________________________
114. What’s the typical telematics data refresh rate?
Standard subscription: 15-minute intervals. Premium: 1-minute. Dealers can modify reporting frequency in VisionLink Configuration Manager.
________________________________________
115. Can a customer export telematics data to third-party systems?
Yes, via VisionLink API or CSV scheduler. Exports must honor Caterpillar’s Data Use Agreement (DUA) ensuring privacy and data-ownership rights.
________________________________________
116. How do dealers analyze machine fuel efficiency trends?
VisionLink Performance Dashboard aggregates fuel consumption (L/h) vs. work hours, compares similar models, and highlights variance beyond ±10 %. Reports can be exported monthly to the dealer portal.
________________________________________
117. What ML techniques are used for failure prediction?
Gradient-boosting ensembles and temporal CNNs trained on event sequences detect deviations in vibration, load, and pressure signals. Confidence > 0.8 triggers a proactive maintenance alert.
________________________________________
118. How are over-the-air (OTA) updates validated?
Each OTA package carries a digital signature verified by the device TPM before installation. Invalid or tampered packages are automatically quarantined.
________________________________________
119. How to manage user access to dealer systems?
Access is governed by Identity and Access Management (IAM) integrated with Cat Dealer Single Sign-On (SSO). Roles include Service Tech, Warranty Admin, Parts Analyst, and Manager.
________________________________________
120. What’s the password policy for SSO accounts?
Minimum 12 characters, mixed case, digit, symbol; expires every 90 days. MFA via mobile app or FIDO2 key is mandatory for admin roles.
________________________________________
121. How frequently should IAM access reviews occur?
Quarterly reviews required under Caterpillar Security Directive ISD-001 (Access Control). Managers must certify or revoke inactive accounts in the Access Review Portal.
________________________________________
122. What audit logs are retained?
Dealer System Audit Logs (DAL) capture user logins, configuration changes, warranty claim edits, and API access events. Logs retained 7 years for compliance.
________________________________________
123. How does Caterpillar detect system anomalies?
Security Operations Center (SOC) runs UEBA models correlating dealer logins, geography, and access patterns. Unusual behavior triggers multi-factor challenge and alert.
________________________________________
124. What backup policy applies to dealer databases?
Daily incremental and weekly full backups encrypted with AES-256 stored in dual cloud regions. Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 8 hours; Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 4 hours.
________________________________________
125. How is data privacy ensured for customer information?
All personal data is tagged under Data Classification ISD-014. Access restricted to “Need to Know.” Transmission uses tokenized IDs to prevent PII exposure.
________________________________________
126. Are dealers required to comply with GDPR or CCPA?
Yes, depending on operating region. Dealer Legal Compliance Team reviews data handling processes annually and ensures opt-out mechanisms are functional.
________________________________________
127. What’s the policy for storing diagnostic data on laptops?
Local storage must be encrypted using BitLocker (AES-256) and auto-delete logs after 30 days. Data should sync to Dealer Central Repository (DCR) nightly.
________________________________________
128. Can dealers use cloud storage for service photos?
Yes, only Caterpillar-approved cloud (e.g., Azure CAT Tenant). Public cloud folders like Dropbox or Google Drive are prohibited per ISD-032.
________________________________________
129. How are firmware signing keys protected?
Keys reside in Hardware Security Modules (HSMs) within Caterpillar PKI infrastructure; only the release manager can sign flash files after QA validation.
________________________________________
130. What risk exists if IAM reviews are skipped?
Unauthorized or dormant accounts may be exploited, leading to data breach. Risk rating: High. Mitigation: Automated quarterly access certification and periodic penetration tests.
________________________________________
131. How are API keys rotated?
Every 90 days automatically through DIG Key Lifecycle Manager. Expired keys block API traffic until new keys are issued.
________________________________________
132. Can technicians use personal devices for diagnostics?
No. Only dealer-issued, encrypted devices with CAT Endpoint Protection (EPP) are allowed. Personal devices violate ISD-021 (Mobile Device Control).
________________________________________
133. What network segmentation rules apply in workshops?
Operational Technology (OT) networks for machines are segmented from IT business networks using firewalls and VLAN policies per ISD-009 (Network Security).
________________________________________
134. How are service laptops patched?
Via Dealer WSUS Server synced weekly to Microsoft Update. Missing patches older than 30 days trigger non-compliance alerts.
________________________________________
135. What vulnerability scanning tools are approved?
Qualys Cloud Agent and CAT Internal Scanner (CIS) must run monthly to detect outdated software, unencrypted volumes, and open ports.
________________________________________
136. How are phishing risks mitigated?
Quarterly awareness campaigns, simulated phishing emails, and enforced MFA. Dealer Security Teams track click-through rates and conduct follow-up training.
________________________________________
137. What’s the escalation path for detected malware?
Isolate endpoint → Run EPP scan → Report incident to Dealer SOC → Submit IOC hash to Caterpillar Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) for analysis.
________________________________________
138. Can dealers run penetration tests?
Yes, with prior authorization from Caterpillar Cybersecurity Office. Results must be submitted within 10 days for remediation review.
________________________________________
139. How to manage remote access vendors?
Third-party access requires short-lived VPN credentials and session recording per ISD-045 (Remote Vendor Management). Access expires after 72 hours.
________________________________________
140. What controls apply to configuration management?
Changes must be logged in Configuration Control System (CCS). All production config changes require dual approval and rollback plan.
________________________________________
141. How are audit results documented?
Each control review is logged in Compliance Evidence Register (CER) with “Compliant / Non-compliant” status and associated evidence files (e.g., screenshots, logs).
________________________________________
142. What’s the retention for audit evidence?
Minimum 7 years or per contractual obligation. Backed up in read-only format with SHA-256 integrity checks.
________________________________________
143. How do dealers report cybersecurity incidents?
Through Incident Response Portal (IRP) within 1 hour of discovery. Severity 1 events require immediate phone notification to CAT SOC Hotline.
________________________________________
144. What’s the average incident response time?
Severity 1: 4 hrs; Severity 2: 24 hrs; Severity 3: 72 hrs. Metrics tracked under ISD-060 (Incident Management Standard).
________________________________________
145. How does Caterpillar validate dealer security maturity?
Annual Dealer Cyber Maturity Assessment (DCMA) scores controls across Identity, Network, Endpoint, and Governance domains.
________________________________________
146. Can dealers benchmark their performance regionally?
Yes. DCMA dashboard shows comparative scores vs. regional and global averages to identify improvement areas.
________________________________________
147. How are outdated diagnostic tools handled?
End-of-Life (EOL) notices are published six months prior. Dealers must migrate to approved versions and dispose of legacy hardware through secure recycling channels.
________________________________________
148. What’s the policy for retaining machine data post-sale?
Machine data is retained for five years after equipment decommission unless customer requests deletion per Data Retention Directive ISD-050.
________________________________________
149. How is dealer compliance verified?
Quarterly self-assessments + annual third-party audits by Caterpillar Compliance Office. Findings are tracked to closure in Remediation Tracker (RT).
________________________________________
150. Where can dealers access all current ISD directives?
Through the Caterpillar Information Security Repository (CISR). Each directive has a summary, implementation guide, and evidence template for compliance validation.
